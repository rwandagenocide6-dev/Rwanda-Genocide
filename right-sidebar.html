Conditions

Compare the Conditions Before and After the Rwanda Genocide

Belgian colonial rulers introduced the ethnic identity card system in 1932 which favoured the tutsis, but when Rwanda got independence and Hutus got in power they instead of destroying the ethnic identity cards they instead kept it to use it against tutsi as anyone whom is a tutsi is banned from receiving higher education,university placement and government jobs. Violent anti tutsi riot and state sponsored/supported massacres happened from time to time. Which led to some tutsis to flee and they later stats a civil war to reclaim their homeland. Radio station were created to broad cast hate, propaganda and discrimination against tutsis. After the Genocide the ethnic identity card were destroyed and it became illegal to publicly announce that you are of either hutu, twa or tutsi ethnic as well as restricted freespeech to avoid further conflicts. Alot of buildings, public facility, and school were destroyed, which led to many people without homes and clean drinking water or food. There were also over 100,000 suspects whom participated in the genocide, which they set up community courts to plead for forgiveness. Now Rwanda is one of Africa's fastest growing country with one of the most modern city and a fix economy.
