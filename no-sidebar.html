Rwandan Genocide: Timeline of Events that took place

Outline the Timeline of events that took place in the Rwanda Genocide

1932

Ethnic identity Cards

Enthic identity/social class cards are introduced to separate hutu, tutsi and twa with tutsi being the ones whom are in favour of the cards.

1959

Hutu Peasant Revolt

The Hutus revolt against the Tutsi and causing 150,000 tutsi to flee to other neighboring countries.

1962

Belgian Grants Independence

Belgian grants Rwanda independence and hutu gained power which they used to establish republic and sponsers anti tutsi discriminations.

1990

Rwandan Patriotic Front(RPF)

A rebel army consisting of Tutsis were created causing civil war.

1993

Hutu Radio channel/station

Hutus starts a radio channel/station that broad cast and spread propaganda against the tutsis.

1994 April 6th

President Juvenal Habyarimana Gets shot down

A plane carrying Hutu President Juvenal Habyarimana gets shot down and kills him with a few others, causing a outrage in the Hutu extremist population in which they blamed it on the Tutsis and RPF dispite the lack of evidence

1994 April 7th

The Start of the Genocide

The hutu army and militia formed by the Hutu extremist sets up road blocks and goes door to door to murder all Tutsis and moderate political leaders as well as anti corruption officers.

1994 April 8th

RPF Assults North Side

RPF(Rwandan Patriotic Front) launches a assult from the north to fight and halt the genocide.

1994 April 21st

Murder of 10 Belgian Peace Keepers

The murder of 10 belgian peace keepers cause UN(United Nations) to pull back the around 2230 peace keepers stationed in Rwanda and leaving around 270 soldiers which led to many victims being unprotected and killed.

1998 April 28th - 30th

The Advancing of the Rwandan Patriotic Front

The RPF advances through the country causing many Hutus to flee to other countries.

1994 Mid of May

Genocide Dies Down

The RPF searches for the lost and dead or injured Tutsis whim fled and hid during the chaos

1994 June 22nd

Safe Zones

The UN establishes safe zones for both Tutsis and Hutus which was controversial as it let some militia and partaker of the genocide escape.

1994 July 4th-19th

Rwanda Genocide Comes to an End

RPF captures the captial city of kigali ending the genocide for good and later declares total victory and end of genocide offically in July 18th-19th
