The Three factions involved

Hutu

Hutu is a native african ethnic group native to africa with them migrating to rwanda 5th to 11th century which they lived with the Twa people and they are around 85% of the population in Rwanda. Before colonisation Hutu were a subjugated social class that worked as farmers and agriculturalists under feudal Tutsi monarchy.

Tutsi

Tutsi are cattle owners they arrived in Rwanda at a later time than Hutu and Twa. They established a feudal like system in which Hutu are to serve under a Tutsi lord whom had more money and power in exchange for a loan on land and cattle.

Twa

Twa is the first native african inhabitants of Rwanda before Tutsi and Hutu. They had hunted and gathered food for a long time before the Tutsi and Hutu arrived but the forest which they did such activities were cut down for land and space causing them to do other jobs such as blacksmithing and pottery when the feudal system was established as they were the lowest social class.

Main Causes

Describe Why the Rwanda Genocide Lasted 100 Days

The constant strain of people spreading propaganda and hate finally snapped creating a genocide that plans to erase Tutsis forever. The international governments and United Nations refuse to act/intervene as well as taking peacekeeping forces back and leaving the genocide to unfold without restrictions and limitations. The Rwanda Patriotic Front(RPF) was the only main hope to stop and end the genocide for most of the 100 Days that the genocide spanned for. United States avoided using the word Genocide to prevent sending in military assitant as 6 months earlier in 1993 18 US soldier were killed in the "Black Hawk Down" incident in Somalia. With little resources to work with the forces that remained in Rwanda had no way to protect all the victims and stop the war which caused Hutu militia and army of Rwanda to run free with killing hundreds of thousands to millions of people.

Voices of the People

Assess How Citizen's Voices affected the Rwanda Genocide

Many innocent civilians didnt support the genocide but, participated as refusal could lead to punishment/torture or worse. Radio stations were established by civilians to rat out and tell everyone of Tutsi whereabouts and hiding spots, the main radio station responsible was the Radio Télévision Libre des Mille Collines (RTLM). Hutus named the mass murder as communal work and civic duty as tradition, Hutus also killed politicians whom spoke out for Tutsis and peace which set and example that speaking for peace is treason which will be punished by death or torture and silenced the government.
